## 1. Overview

The Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA) (20 U.S.C. 1232g; 34 CFR Part 99) protects the privacy of student education records. This document describes how Bhapi Inc. ("Bhapi") complies with FERPA when providing AI safety monitoring services to K-12 schools and districts through the bhapi.ai platform.

---

## 2. Bhapi's Role Under FERPA

### 2.1 School Official Exception

Bhapi operates as a "school official" with a "legitimate educational interest" under FERPA 34 CFR 99.31(a)(1)(i)(B). This designation is established through our Student Data Privacy Agreement (SDPA) with each school or district, which specifies that:

- Bhapi performs an institutional service or function for which the school would otherwise use employees.
- Bhapi is under the direct control of the school with respect to the use and maintenance of education records.
- Bhapi uses education records only for the purposes for which access was granted.
- Bhapi meets the school's criteria for "school official" as stated in its annual FERPA notification.

### 2.2 Scope of Service

Bhapi provides AI safety monitoring — not instruction, grading, or curriculum delivery. Our access to student data is limited to:

- AI platform interaction metadata (timestamps, platform used, session duration).
- AI conversation content captured by the browser extension (for safety analysis only).
- Risk scores and safety alerts generated by the Bhapi risk pipeline.
- Student identifiers necessary to associate monitoring with the correct student.

## 3. Data Classification

| Classification | Examples | Handling |
|---------------|----------|----------|
| **Directory Information** | Student name, grade level, school enrollment | May be disclosed per school's directory info policy. Bhapi treats as confidential regardless. |
| **Personally Identifiable Information (PII)** | Student ID, email, date of birth, parent/guardian contact | Encrypted at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.3). Access restricted to authorized roles. |
| **Education Records** | AI interaction logs associated with a student, risk assessments, safety alerts | Protected under FERPA. Access limited to school officials and authorized Bhapi personnel. Not disclosed without consent or legal authority. |
| **De-identified / Aggregate Data** | Aggregate risk trends, platform usage statistics (no student identifiers) | May be used for product improvement and reporting. Cannot be re-identified. |

## 4. Access Controls

### 4.1 Role-Based Access

| Role | Access Level |
|------|-------------|
| **School Admin** | Full access to their school's student data, risk reports, alerts, and configuration. Can manage teacher/staff accounts. |
| **Teacher/Staff** | Access to students assigned to their classes/groups only. Cannot modify school-wide settings. |
| **Parent/Guardian** | Access to their own child's data only (when school has enabled family portal). |
| **Student** | Access to their own child-appropriate dashboard only (age-gated, COPPA-compliant for under 13). |
| **Bhapi Support** | Access only when requested by school admin for troubleshooting. All access is logged and time-limited. |
| **Bhapi Engineering** | No access to production student data without explicit authorization and audit logging. |

### 4.2 Technical Controls

- Multi-tenant isolation: each school's data is logically isolated at the database level.
- Role-based access control (RBAC) enforced at the API layer.
- MFA required for all admin accounts.
- Session timeouts (configurable by school admin, default 30 minutes).
- All data access is logged with correlation IDs for audit.

---

## 5. Data Retention and Deletion

### 5.1 During Active Contract

- AI interaction data: retained for the current school year plus one prior year (configurable by school admin).
- Risk assessments and alerts: retained for the current school year plus one prior year.
- Audit logs: retained for 3 years.

### 5.2 Upon Contract Termination

- School admin may export all data prior to termination.
- All student education records are deleted within 30 days of contract termination.
- De-identified aggregate data may be retained for product improvement.
- Deletion is confirmed in writing to the school district.
- Backup data is purged within 90 days per backup rotation schedule.

### 5.3 Student Departure

- When a school admin removes a student from the platform, that student's data is marked for deletion.
- Data is deleted within 30 days of removal.
- School admin receives confirmation of deletion.

## 6. Annual Notification Template

Schools are required to provide annual FERPA notification to parents. Below is a template paragraph schools may include in their annual notification to cover Bhapi's role:

> **AI Safety Monitoring Service**
>
> [School/District Name] uses Bhapi (bhapi.ai), an AI safety monitoring service, to help ensure student safety when using AI platforms for educational purposes. Bhapi operates as a school official with a legitimate educational interest under FERPA. Bhapi monitors student interactions with AI platforms (such as ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, and others) to detect safety risks including inappropriate content, emotional dependency, and academic integrity concerns. Bhapi does not use student data for marketing or advertising purposes. Student data is encrypted and access is limited to authorized school personnel and Bhapi staff operating under the school's direction. Parents may review their child's AI safety data by contacting [school contact] or through the parent portal at bhapi.ai. For questions about this service, contact [school contact information].

## 7. Breach Notification

### 7.1 Definition

A "breach" under this policy means any unauthorized access, disclosure, or loss of student education records or PII.

### 7.2 Notification Timeline

| Step | Timeline | Action |
|------|----------|--------|
| **Detection** | Immediate | Bhapi's incident response team investigates the potential breach. |
| **Internal Assessment** | < 24 hours | Determine scope, affected records, and root cause. |
| **School Notification** | < 72 hours | Notify affected school district(s) in writing with details of the breach, data affected, and remediation steps. |
| **Parent Notification** | Per school direction | School determines whether and how to notify parents. Bhapi supports with template language and data. |
| **Regulatory Notification** | As required | Comply with state breach notification laws (varies by jurisdiction). |
| **Post-Incident Report** | < 30 days | Provide detailed incident report to affected schools. |

## 8. Parent and Guardian Rights

Under FERPA, parents (and eligible students aged 18+) have the following rights with respect to education records held by Bhapi:

### 8.1 Right to Access

- Parents may request to inspect and review their child's AI monitoring data.
- Requests can be made through the parent portal or by contacting the school admin.
- Bhapi will provide access within 45 days of the request (FERPA maximum).

### 8.2 Right to Amendment

- Parents may request amendment of records they believe are inaccurate or misleading.
- Amendment requests are submitted to the school admin, who coordinates with Bhapi.
- If the request is denied, the parent has the right to a hearing and to place a statement in the record.

### 8.3 Right to Consent

- FERPA generally requires prior written consent before disclosing PII from education records.
- Exceptions include the school official exception (under which Bhapi operates) and other FERPA-permitted disclosures.
- Bhapi does not disclose student data to third parties outside the exceptions without school or parent consent.

### 8.4 Right to File a Complaint

- Parents may file a complaint with the U.S. Department of Education:
  - Family Policy Compliance Office (FPCO)
  - U.S. Department of Education
  - 400 Maryland Avenue, SW
  - Washington, DC 20202
